If you're asking "who's Italy's prime minister?", the answer is Giorgia Meloni. She assumed office on October 22, 2022, making history as the first woman to hold Italy's top governmental role. Hailing from a background of right-wing activism, Meloni has charted a remarkable course from a youth organizer to the head of government of the Eurozone's third-largest economy. ### Understanding the Background of Italy's Prime Minister

To truly understand who Italy's prime minister is, one must look at her extensive political history. Giorgia Meloni's journey began long before she entered the Palazzo Chigi, the prime minister's official residence. Born in Rome in 1977, she became politically active in her teenage years. At just 15, she joined the Youth Front, the youth wing of the neo-fascist Italian Social Movement (MSI). This early start in politics shaped her ideological foundation and set her on a path of national-conservative activism. Throughout the 1990s, she rose through the ranks of successor right-wing movements, eventually becoming the national leader of Student Action, the student movement of the National Alliance party.

Her career took a significant step forward in 2006 when she was elected to the Italian Chamber of Deputies for the first time. Just two years later, in 2008, she was appointed Minister for Youth in Silvio Berlusconi's fourth government. At 31, she became the youngest minister in the history of the Italian Republic, a testament to her rapid ascent. A defining moment in her career came in 2012 when she co-founded the Brothers of Italy party, a political force born from a split with Berlusconi's People of Freedom party. She became its president in 2014, molding the party around her vision of national identity, traditional values, and sovereignty. According to [biographical information from the Italian government](https://www.governo.it/it/presidente/biografia), her leadership steadily grew the party's profile from a small political entity into a major contender in national politics.
